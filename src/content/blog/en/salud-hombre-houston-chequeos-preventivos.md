Many men only see a doctor when something already hurts. The problem is that high blood pressure, diabetes and prostate changes progress silently for years without causing discomfort. By the time symptoms appear, the damage is usually more advanced and treatment becomes more complicated. The good news: a simple preventive checkup, once or twice a year, can catch these problems early, while they are still easy to control.

## Why should men get regular checkups?

The statistics are clear: men visit the doctor far less than women, and Hispanic men working long shifts tend to postpone their health even more. An annual checkup helps you:

- **Detect early** high blood pressure, diabetes and high cholesterol, before they damage your heart or kidneys
- **Monitor your prostate** with a simple blood test (PSA)
- **Understand your energy and mood**, which often depend on your testosterone level
- **Avoid costly complications**: prevention always costs less than treating an emergency
- **Get peace of mind** knowing how your body is really doing

## Warning signs: don't ignore them

Come to the clinic as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, or shortness of breath with exertion
- Difficulty urinating, weak stream, or getting up several times at night
- Blood in your urine
- Excessive thirst, constant hunger or frequent urination
- Persistent headaches or blurry vision
- Swelling in your legs or ankles
- Extreme fatigue or changes in sexual desire
- Lumps or pain in the testicles

## Practical tips to care for your health

- Get a general checkup **at least once a year**, even if you feel fine
- Walk or exercise 30 minutes a day, even at home
- Cut back on sodas, fried food and excess salt
- Sleep 7–8 hours: rest also regulates blood pressure and testosterone
- If you smoke, ask for help quitting; heart risk drops from the first month
- Don't self-medicate: the wrong antibiotic or supplement can mask the real problem
